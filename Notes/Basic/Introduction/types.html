Classification of Computers
Classification Criteria
Computers are classified in two main ways:
  1. On the basis of Work/Function
  2. On the basis of Size
Based on Work / Function
Analog Computer
Analog Computers process continuous data and represent physical quantities such as temperature, speed, voltage, or pressure. They provide results as readings on dials, meters, or graphs. Used in science, engineering, and industry for real-time measurements and simulations.
Examples: Thermometers, speedometers, voltmeters, analog clocks.
🔒
Digital Computer
Digital Computers process data in binary form (0 and 1). They perform calculations, logical operations, and data processing using electronic circuits. Most modern computers (PCs, laptops, smartphones) are digital.
Examples: IBM PC, Apple Macintosh, modern smartphones, calculators.
🔒
Hybrid Computer
Hybrid Computers combine the features of both analog and digital computers. They can process both continuous (analog) and discrete (digital) data. Used in specialized applications like hospital monitoring systems, petrol pumps, and scientific research.
Example: Petrol pump computers measure fuel quantity (analog) and calculate price (digital).
🔒
Based on Size
Micro Computer
Micro Computers are the smallest and most widely used computers. They use microprocessors as their CPU and are commonly used at homes, schools, and small offices. Also known as Personal Computers (PCs).
Subtypes: Desktop, Laptop, Palmtop, Notebook, Tablet
🔒
Desktop Computer
Desktop Computers are designed for regular use at a single location. They include a CPU, monitor, keyboard, and mouse, and are placed on a desk. Desktops are larger and require a constant power supply.
🔒
Laptop Computer
Laptop Computers are portable and compact, with all components integrated into a single device. They run on battery power and are ideal for mobile use.
🔒
Palmtop, Notebook & Tablet Computers
Palmtop Computers are smaller than laptops and can fit in your palm. Notebook Computers are similar to laptops but generally lighter and thinner. Tablet Computers are touch-screen devices, larger than smartphones but smaller than laptops, and highly portable.
🔒
Mini Computer
Mini Computers are mid-sized computers used mainly in organizations for tasks like managing databases and serving multiple users simultaneously. They are more powerful than microcomputers but smaller than mainframes.
Example: PDP-8 (developed by DEC in 1965)
🔒
Mainframe Computer
Mainframe Computers are large, powerful systems used by big organizations for bulk data processing and supporting hundreds of users at the same time. They offer high memory and processing capabilities.
🔒
Super Computer
Super Computers are the fastest and most powerful computers, used for complex scientific calculations, weather forecasting, nuclear research, and high-end graphics. They use multiple CPUs working together in parallel.
World's First Super Computer: CDC 6600
India's First Super Computer: PARAM 8000
Applications: Space research, defense, weather forecasting, scientific simulations, and high-resolution movie production.
